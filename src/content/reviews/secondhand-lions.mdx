On a whim, I watched this with the wife one night, without knowing anything about it except that it stars Michael Caine and a teenage Haley Joel Osment.

What I didn't expect was to be totally riveted throughout the entire runtime - not a single glance at my phone. We were never quite sure where the movie was going, and we had to know.

## The emotional rollercoaster

Secondhand Lions seems well aware of its potential intended audience - families. Family movies have a well established convention of what is allowed - Secondhand Lions confidently walks up to the very edge of what is allowed, and stays there. We get to explore disbelief, hope, betrayal, inspiration. We see abuse and disappointment and fear. It swings from emotion to emotion, while keeping a tone of warmth throughout. It's a true family movie for everyone, not just the kids, while not insulting the intelligence of anyone.

## A speech for young men

I'm an atheist, but this movie contains a speech so well written and well delivered it had me thinking about the meaning of belief, and how I in fact believe in things too, just not necessarily God.

I'll say no more - go watch it.
