# Building a Home Theatre: Lessons from a Second-Hand Audio Journey

> **TL;DR:** Inspired by Linus Tech Tips' "$250 surround sound" video, the author built a 5.1 system from second-hand parts. **Total cost: $375.10** (with ~$126 wasted on defective gear). **25% failure rate** on used items. Result: vastly superior audio with unique spatial experiences, but required significant time, space, and patience.

---

## The Problem: Why Upgrade?

**Previous setup:** $110 soundbar (Nova S50) with 5" "subwoofer"
- Constant volume fussing during movies
- Dialog unintelligible or coming from wrong speaker
- "Plasticky cheap defeat" — author rates it 3⭐ vs Amazon's 4.2⭐
- Never a "sit down and enjoy" experience

**Catalyst:** [Linus Tech Tips: "I Built an INSANE Surround Sound Setup for Under $250"](https://www.youtube.com/embed/u4LFDPbbSVk?si=o3B-mEfXjgwf7QfI)

---

## The Second-Hand Reality Check

### Failure Rate: **25% (3 of 12 items defective)**

| Defective Item | Issue | Seller Response |
|----------------|-------|-----------------|
| Subwoofer | Too quiet to use | No refund |
| Receiver #1 | Powered on, no sound | Refused to test beforehand |
| Receiver #2 | Worked initially, then failed | No refund |

> **"For 95% of defective items, sellers won't refund you."**

### Hidden Costs of Buying Used
- **Time wasted:** Hours communicating, driving, diagnosing, negotiating, disposing
- **Testing resistance:** 2/3 sellers refused to test ("time not worth it for cheap items")
- **Disposal burden:** Had to attend electronics recycling event

## Space Requirements (Overlooked Factor)

- **Floor space:** Subwoofer claims significant room
- **Entertainment unit:** Receiver + speakers displace decor (wife's plants)
- **Garage:** 100-ft speaker wire spool — "will likely never use"

| **Subwoofer** | [NHT Verve subwoofer](https://github.com/hungsu/digital_garden/tree/main/src/site/notes/Gear/NHT%20Verve%20subwoofer.md) | ⭐ Star performer | Dual 10" woofers |

**Placement note:** Speakers partially obscured by TV — author hasn't tested if moving them improves sound.

## The Payoff: Unique Audio Experiences

> **"I've had some unique audio experiences that I don't think I've had before:"**

| Media | Moment | Spatial Effect |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| *Devil May Cry (2025)* | Tentacle moving across screen | Sound tracked visual position — "coming from the tentacle on screen!" |
| *Last of Us S2* | Therapist calling from another room | **Thought someone was in the house** |
| *Mr Holmes* | Night scenes with insects | Felt irritation at "real" buzzing insects |

> **Comparison:** Author has worn many headphones and visited IMAX theatres — **this setup delivered spatial realism neither could**.

## Verdict & Next Steps

### Would do it again? **YES.**

### Current Upgrade Hunger
- **Onkyo L/R speakers** are the weak link — actively seeking replacements
- Centre (NHT M5) and sub (NHT Verve) are keepers

---

## Key Takeaways for Aspiring Builders

| Factor | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| **Budget** | Plan for 30-50% over target for failed purchases + cables |
| **Time** | Weeks of hunting, testing, returns, disposal |
| **Risk** | ~25% failure rate; minimal seller accountability |
| **Space** | Measure *before* buying — sub + receiver + wires add up |
| **Reward** | Spatial audio impossible on soundbars/headphones |

> **Bottom line:** Second-hand home theatre delivers **exceptional value per dollar** but demands **patience, space, and tolerance for failure**. The audio payoff is genuine and distinct from both soundbars and headphones.
