## The Journey: Phase by Phase

### Phase 1: The Receiver Hunt (Months 1-3)

**Target**: Denon X3700H / X4700H / Marantz SR7015 — 9ch processing, 11ch pre-out, HDMI 2.1, Dirac Live ready.

| Listing | Price | Condition | Outcome |
|---------|-------|-----------|---------|
| X3700H, "barely used" | $650 | Mint, box + remote | **Bought** |
| X4700H, "HDMI board replaced" | $700 | Repaired | Passed — risk |
| SR7015, "no remote" | $600 | Good | Passed — remote $80 |

**Lesson**: Denon X3700H is the sweet spot. 9ch amp + 11ch processing = 5.1.2 with external amp for heights (or use Zone 2). HDMI 2.1 ports (3×) handle PS5 / Xbox Series X / PC.

**Gotcha**: "HDMI 2.1" on 2020 models = 40Gbps (not 48Gbps). 4K120 4:4:4 works, 8K/4K120 12-bit needs 48Gbps. Fine for gaming, not for future 8K.

### Phase 2: Front Stage — The Monitor Audio Decision (Months 3-6)

**Why Monitor Audio Silver?**
- Neutral, detailed sound signature (matches my preference)
- Attractive real-wood veneers (walnut = wife approved)
- Strong second-hand market in UK/AU/US
- Silver 300 (floorstanders) + C250 centre = timbre-matched

**The Hunt:**

| Speaker | Listings Watched | Price Range | Final |
|---------|------------------|-------------|-------|
| Silver 300 (pair) | 12 | $450-700 | $500 |
| Silver C250 | 8 | $150-250 | $180 |
| Silver FX (surrounds) | 5 | $200-350 | $220 |

**Lesson**: **Patience pays**. The Silver 300s appeared 3× over 4 months. First two were damaged / overpriced. The $500 pair was from a dealer trade-in — professional photos, original boxes, transferable warranty.

**Mistake**: Initially wanted Silver 500 (larger, more bass). Realised in-room, the 300s + SB-2000 Pro integrate better. Crossover at 80Hz → sub handles &lt;80Hz, 300s shine at mid/high.

### Phase 3: Surrounds & Heights (Months 6-10)

**Surrounds**: Monitor Audio Silver FX (bipole/dipole switchable)
- **Why bipole?** Diffuse soundfield for movies, multiple seating positions
- **Mounting**: Wall-mounted at ear height + 30cm, angled down
- **Switch**: Left on "bipole" — dipole made surrounds too diffuse for Atmos

**Heights**: Polk Audio OWM3 (wall-mountable, angled)
- **Why Polk?** $40/ea used, 4" driver + 0.75" tweeter, keyhole mount
- **Placement**: Front height (not top middle) — Dolby recommends front height for 5.1.2
- **Angle**: Aimed at MLP (main listening position)

| Height Speaker Option | Cost (pair) | Mounting | Atmos Performance |
|----------------------|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| Polk OWM3 (used) | $80 | Keyhole, flexible | Good |
| Monitor Audio Silver 100 (used) | $300 | Stand/wall | Better timbre match |
| KEQ Q50a (Atmos modules) | $250 | Top of fronts | Convenient |
| DIY in-ceiling | $200+ | Permanent | Best |

**Lesson**: Front height > top middle for 5.1.2. Top middle needs 4 height channels (5.1.4) to image properly. OWM3s punch above their weight.

### Phase 4: The Subwoofer — SVS SB-2000 Pro (Months 10-14)

**Why sealed?**
- Music-first listening (sealed = tighter, faster bass)
- Room size: 55m³ — SB-2000 Pro rated for 40-60m³
- Aesthetic: Cube format, fits beside media unit
- App control: DSP, room gain compensation, presets

**Hunt**: SB-2000 Pro rarely appears used. Waited 4 months. Snagged one from an upgraders to dual SB-3000s.

**Integration**: SVS app → Room Gain Compensation (RGC) set to "Medium" (corner placement). Crossover 80Hz, phase 0°, volume -3dB from auto-calibration.

**Verification**: REW (Room EQ Wizard) + UMIK-1 measurement mic confirmed flat response to 22Hz (-3dB). Happy.

### Phase 5: Calibration & Tuning (Months 14-18)

**Audyssey MultEQ XT32 (Denon X3700H)**
- 8-position measurement
- **Result**: Good starting point, but...
- **Issues**: Over-boosted sub (house curve too aggressive), heights set to "Large" (wrong), centre -2dB too hot

**Manual Tuning**:
1. **Sub**: Disabled Audyssey sub EQ → used SVS app RGC + manual -3dB
2. **Heights**: Set to "Small", 80Hz crossover (OWM3s roll off ~80Hz)
3. **Centre**: -1dB trim (dialogue clarity)
4. **Surrounds**: +1dB (bipole dispersion loses perceived level)
5. **Target Curve**: "Reference" → "Flat" for music, "Reference" for movies

**Dirac Live?** X3700H is "Dirac Ready" — license $349. **Skipped**. Audyssey XT32 + manual tweaks + SVS app = 90% there. Diminishing returns.

## Mistakes & What I'd Do Differently

| Mistake | Cost | Lesson |
|---------|------|--------|
| Bought Silver FX surrounds before confirming bipole vs dipole preference | $0 (kept) | Test dipole/bipole in-room — room acoustics decide |
| Didn't measure room modes before sub placement | Time | Sub crawl + REW first, then buy |
| Overlooked HDMI 2.1 40Gbps vs 48Gbps limitation | Future-proofing | Fine for now, but limits 4K120 12-bit HDR |
| Skipped Dirac Live | $349 saved | Would try if starting over — XT32 is good, Dirac is better |
| Height speakers not timbre-matched to fronts | Minor | Audible on pans — would stretch for Silver 100s used |

## Second-Hand Buying Tips (Hard-Won)

1. **Dealer trade-ins > private sellers** — tested, warrantied, packed properly
2. **Ask for original boxes** — shipping speakers without OEM boxes = damage risk
3. **Test before buying (local)** — bring a phone + 3.5mm cable, play test tones
4. **Search broadly** — "Monitor Audio Silver" not just "Silver 300"
5. **Set alerts** — FB Marketplace, eBay saved searches, hifi forums (AVS, AVForums, Reddit r/avexchange)
6. **Negotiate with data** — "Sold listings show $X, I'll do $Y cash today"
7. **Factor shipping** — $50-150 for speakers, $80-200 for AVRs/subwoofers

## The Experience: Movies, Music, Gaming

### Movies (4K Blu-ray / Streaming)
- **Dune (2021)**: Ornithopter wings overhead — first time Atmos *clicked*
- **Top Gun: Maverick**: Jet passes — visceral, positional
- **Streaming (Disney+/Netflix)**: Atmos via eARC works, but bitrate lower than disc

### Music (Roon → Denon HEOS / AirPlay 2)
- **Stereo direct mode**: Bypasses Audyssey, pure 2.1 — stunning with Silver 300s
- **Multi-channel music** (Steven Wilson, Blu-ray Audio): Surrounds add space without gimmick

### Gaming (PS5 / PC)
- **PS5 Tempest 3D Audio → Atmos**: Works via LPCM 5.1/7.1, not true Atmos but effective
- **PC (Windows Sonic / Dolby Atmos for Headphones)**: Different use case
- **Latency**: Denon "Game" mode (low latency) — no perceptible lag

## Would I Recommend This Path?

**Yes, if:**
- You have **time** (6-18 months) and **patience**
- You enjoy the **hunt** and learning process
- You can **measure and tune** (REW, UMIK-1, Audyssey)
- You want **high-end performance at mid-fi prices**

**No, if:**
- You need it **working this weekend**
- You want **timbre-matched new** with warranty
- You don't want to **debug HDMI handshake issues** at 11pm
- **Wife acceptance factor** requires invisible speakers (in-wall/in-ceiling)
