# Building a Home Theatre: Lessons from a Second-Hand Audio Journey

> **TL;DR:** Inspired by Linus Tech Tips, the author built a 5.1 home theatre system using second-hand gear. Despite a 25% failure rate, $126 in wasted purchases, and space constraints, the final $375 setup delivers exceptional audio—surpassing soundbars and even some theatre experiences.

---

## The Problem: Soundbar Frustration

- **Soundbar:** $110 Nova S50 (4.2⭐ Amazon, author rates ~3⭐)
- **Constant issues during movie nights:**
  - "Can we turn on subtitles?"
  - "Can you make it louder? We can't understand what they're saying"
  - "It's too loud, can you turn it back down?"
- **Result:** Never a "sit down and enjoy" experience; always fussing with volume, never 100% focused on the movie
- **Audio quality:** "Plasticky cheap defeat" — dialog sometimes came from the pathetic 5" "subwoofer" instead of the soundbar

---

## The Inspiration

**Linus Tech Tips video:** *"I Built an INSANE Surround Sound Setup for Under $250"*
- Promise: Better audio for a few hundred dollars (or $40 for noticeable improvement)
- Plan: Find second-hand speakers + AV receiver locally for up to a few hundred dollars

---

## The Second-Hand Reality Check

### High Failure Rate (25%)

| Failed Item | Issue |
|-------------|-------|
| Subwoofer | Too quiet to practically use |
| Receiver #1 | Turned on but produced no sound |
| Receiver #2 | Worked initially, then failed soon after |

**3 out of 12 items failed** — a shockingly high rate.

### Seller Resistance to Testing
- After first failure, author asked every seller to test items before purchase
- **2 out of 3 refused** — "time not worth it for such cheap items"
- **95% of defective items:** No refunds offered

### Wasted Time (Many Hours)
- Communicating with sellers
- Driving to pick up equipment
- Diagnosing/attempting repairs
- Negotiating refunds (failing)
- Searching for replacements
- Disposing of dead gear (electronics recycling event)

## Space Considerations

- Speakers, receiver, and subwoofer consume **significant floor/entertainment unit space**
- Wife lost space for plants on entertainment unit
- Subwoofer claims floor space
- **Leftover:** Huge spool of speaker wire (100 ft) sitting in garage — likely never to be fully used

## Final Setup (The Winners)

| Component | Model | Role |
|-----------|-------|------|
| **⭐ Star** | **NHT M5 Monitor** | Centre channel speaker |
| **⭐ Star** | **NHT Verve Subwoofer** | Dual 10" woofers |
| **Receiver** | **Denon AVR-S510BT** | AV receiver |
| **L/R Speakers** | Onkyo mini towers (leftover from 7.1 system) | Left/Right channels — *noted as far worse than centre* |

> **Placement note:** Speakers partially obscured by TV — author hasn't tested if moving them improves sound, but "they still sound great"

## Verdict

> **"Would I do this all again? YES."**

### Caveats & Next Steps
- Onkyo L/R speakers sound **far worse than the NHT M5 centre** — already hunting for replacements
- Second-hand route involves **real risk, time, and hidden costs** — not for everyone
- But the **audio payoff is genuine and exceptional**

---

## Key Takeaways for Anyone Considering This Path

1. **Budget 50% more than you think** — cables, failures, and waste add up
2. **Test before buying** — insist on it; walk away if seller refuses
3. **Accept no returns** — factor failure cost into your budget
4. **Measure your space** — subwoofer + receiver + speakers = real footprint
5. **Centre channel matters most** — invest there first (NHT M5 = standout)
6. **L/R matching matters** — mismatched speakers create obvious quality gaps
7. **The result can beat theatres** — for immersive, positional audio at home
